The REAP Project (Realising the Potential of Australia's High Capacity Students) was a project undertaken by the Melbourne Graduate School of Education, The University of Melbourne 2015 - 2023.

This research was supported under the Australian Research Council's Linkage Projects funding scheme (LP140100236, Realising the Potential of Australia's High Capacity Students), in partnership with the State of Victoria as represented by the Department of Education and Training.

Research Invitation

How can schools ensure that they develop and extend all students in their classes?

Research indicates that students at the higher end of their class (described as high achieving or high capacity students) make less progress on average than their less advanced peers. Researchers at the University of Melbourne have developed pilot instruments that measure the aspects of classroom climate that foster the learning growth of high-achieving students, together with teacher learning progressions that show teachers how well their classroom climate currently supports the learning of these students, and what steps teachers can take next to strengthen their practice.

We are seeking teachers from your school to participate in this important PhD project by completing an online questionnaire about classroom climate in their classes.

The online questionnaire consists of 61 multiple choice questions and takes around 15 minutes to complete. Interested teachers who complete the survey are also able to volunteer for an optional 30-minute confidential telephone interview exploring classroom climate in greater depth. We are happy to provide your school with a brief report on the findings at the completion of the project if you would like.
